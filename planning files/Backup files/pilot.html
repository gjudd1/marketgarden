The Market Garden Pilot

One acre. Three women. Folkston, Georgia.

How It Started

Starting Small on Purpose

We started small on purpose. The pilot garden at the Farm at Okefenokee is designed to validate yields, costs, processes, and flows before we scale to 100 acres. Three women from Trinity Rescue Mission's residential program currently work the garden several days a week.

Where We Are

Progress to Date

  • Soil prep and testing have been completed
  • Irrigation has been installed and is operational
  • The one-acre garden has been fully planted with heirloom seeds started in the Okefenokee Farm greenhouse
  • Three women from Trinity Rescue Mission are gaining farming skills and work experience through this employment opportunity
  • The broader Farm at Okefenokee has donated eggs, produce, and meat out of its abundance — food for more than 6,100 meals to date
The Budget

Market Garden Pilot Budget

The Market Garden Pilot budget is $300,000, covering the cost of wages, transportation, housing, and master planning.

Category Purpose
Wages Compensation for women working the garden
Transportation Travel between Jacksonville and Folkston
Housing On-site accommodations during work periods
Master Planning Design and planning for full 100-acre scale
Total $300,000
What's Next

The Road Ahead

Working the pilot project through a full growing cycle, validating the results, completing master planning, and exploring grant opportunities are the steps being taken during this initial phase.

The goal: prove the model at one acre, then scale to 100.
