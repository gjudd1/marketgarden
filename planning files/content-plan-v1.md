## Page 2: The Project (project.html)

### Section 1: The Pilot

We started with 1 acre at the Farm at Okefenokee in Folkston, Georgia. Three women from Trinity Rescue Mission's residential program work the garden 2 days a week. The garden has been fully planted.

The pilot has one job right now: validate yields, costs, processes, and flows before we scale.

### Section 2: Early Results

- 6,100 meals provided to residents at Trinity Rescue Mission
- Women in the program are gaining real farming skills and work experience
- The Farm at Okefenokee has generously shared produce and eggs from the broader farm operation

## Page 3: The Partnership (partnership.html)

### Intro paragraph

Jeff Meyer had a vision: feed people nutritious food, give jobs to women in recovery, and model a close-knit, community-based way of life where people work the land and support one another. Trinity Rescue Mission had the people and the program. The overlap was obvious.

### What the Farm at Okefenokee Brings

- 700+ acres of regenerative farming community in Folkston, Georgia
- Land, equipment, expertise, and an established farming operation
- Jeff Meyer's vision and long-term commitment
- $200,000+ in initial funding and infrastructure (soil preparation, irrigation, testing)

### What Trinity Rescue Mission Brings

- A residential recovery program for women experiencing homelessness
- A structure built around addiction recovery, life skills, and employment readiness
- 6-24 month programs that give women the stability to learn and work
- Connection to the people the project is designed to serve

### Why It Works

Regenerative farming requires patience, consistency, and showing up every day. So does recovery. The two fit together in a way that isn't forced.

## Page 4: About (about.html)

### Jeff Meyer

Jeff Meyer is an entrepreneur, conservationist, and philanthropist who has spent more than 5 decades working on land stewardship, business innovation, and community development. He grew up in a farming community in Iowa.

He's co-founder of the Farm at Okefenokee, a 700+ acre regenerative farming community in Folkston, Georgia. He also founded Johnny Appleseed Organic, an agricultural company focused on environmentally responsible products and climate-positive farming.

His conservation work includes participation in planting 20 million trees across the United States and developing one of the largest privately funded habitat conservation banks in the country — preserving more than 600,000 acres of wildlife habitat.

In the energy sector, Jeff has led large-scale solar development in Texas and clean-energy infrastructure projects serving metropolitan power grids.

He lives in both Georgia and Jacksonville, Florida. Jeff considers it a blessing to work with Trinity Rescue Mission.

### Trinity Rescue Mission

Trinity Rescue Mission is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit focused on ending the cycle of homelessness for men, women, and children across northeast Florida.

Over the last 12 months:
- 194,174 meals distributed
- 2,193 people assisted
- 67,424 nights of shelter provided
- 89 individuals connected to employment

Their programs go beyond emergency shelter. Residents in the 6-24 month transition program get housing, addiction recovery support, life skills training, and employment assistance — the foundation the Market Garden Project is built on.

Learn more at trinityrescue.org.
