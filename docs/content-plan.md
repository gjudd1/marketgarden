### Vision Statement (2-3 sentences)

The Regenerative Farm Initiative is a partnership between The Farm at Okefenokee and Trinity Rescue Mission, designed to provide holistic education for children, create life-giving job opportunities for women and single mothers, and produce nutritious food to feed the homeless of Jacksonville, Florida.

### What the Farm Will Be

- Produce and livestock at scale
- Greenhouse and aquaponics facility
- Canning operation
- Training and education space
- On-site housing for program participants

### Practices that Sustain

Holistic Education for Children
Investing in the future by providing children with the education they need to break the generational cycle of unhealth. Education can enable children to thrive and live an abundant life.
Access to Nutrient-Rich Food
Real nutrition starts with real food. Those who are homeless lack consistent access to the whole, minimally processed foods that form the foundation of physical health, immune function, and long-term vitality. This initiative delivers regenerative, nutrient-dense food to those who need it most.
Jobs for Women and Single Mothers
Creating life giving jobs for women and single mothers in a safe environment where they can disconnect from the chaos and step into stability, mentorship, and structure. Through training at Okefenokee Farm’s established farm-to-table model, the fruit of their labor becomes regenerative food that helps feed guests at Trinity Rescue Mission in downtown Jacksonville.

### The Unique Model
This initiative is designed so that the jobs created for women directly produce food that feeds the tables at Trinity Rescue Mission. This win-win approach allows the women to tangibly see the work they are doing matters all while creating a safe environment to grow and develop life long skills. In addition to the work the women are doing, children will have access to farm education that goes beyond classroom learning, but through various outlets, teaches principles that help instill long-term sustainable growth.

## Page 2: The Market Garden Pilot (pilot.html)

### Section 1: How It Started

One acre. Three women. Folkston, Georgia.

We started small on purpose. The pilot garden at the Farm at Okefenokee is designed to validate yields, costs, processes, and flows before we scale to 100 acres. Three women from Trinity Rescue Mission's residential program currently work the garden several days a week. 

### Section 2: Where We Are

- Soil prep and testing have been completed
- Irrigation has been installed and is operational
- The one acre garden has been fully planted with heirloom seeds that started in the Okefenokee Farm greenhouse.
- Three women from Trinity Rescue Mission have been gaining farming skills and work experience through this employment opportunity
- The broader Farm at Okefenokee has been able to donate eggs, produce and meat out of its abundance. To date, food for more than 6,100 meals have been provided. 

### Section 3: The Budget

The Market Garden Pilot budget is $300,000, covering the cost of wages, transportation, equipment, housing and master planning.

### Section 4: What's Next

Working the pilot project through the growing cycle, validating the results, master planning and grant exploration are all steps that will be taken during the initial phase.

## Page 3: The Partnership (partnership.html)

### Intro

Jeff Meyer had a vision: feed people, employ women in recovery, and build a community rooted in land and work. Trinity Rescue Mission had the people and the program. 

### The Farm at Okefenokee

- 1000+ acres of regenerative farming community in Folkston, Georgia
- Land, equipment, expertise, and an established farming operation
- Jeff Meyer's long-term commitment and vision.

Learn more at okefarm.com
### Trinity Rescue Mission

- Residential recovery program for women experiencing homelessness
- 6-24 month programs built around addiction recovery, life skills, and employment
- Northeast Florida's largest provider of emergency shelter

Over the last 12 months:
- 194,174 meals distributed
- 2,193 people assisted
- 67,424 nights of shelter provided
- 89 individuals connected to employment

Learn more at trinityrescue.org.

### Why It Works

Regenerative farming requires patience, consistency, and showing up every day. So does recovery. 

### Jeff Meyer

Jeff Meyer is co-founder of the Farm at Okefenokee and founder of Johnny Appleseed Organic. His work spans land stewardship, clean energy, and conservation — including 20 million trees planted and 600,000+ acres of wildlife habitat preserved. He grew up in a farming community in Iowa and lives in Georgia and Jacksonville, Florida.
